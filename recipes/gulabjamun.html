Gulab Jamun Recipe

Description:

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from khoya (milk solids) and soaked in sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water. These soft, round sweets are a festive favorite, often served during celebrations and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon rose water
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Chopped nuts for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking soda to form a dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a marble).
  3. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying.
  4. Fry the balls on low heat until golden brown, ensuring even cooking.
  5. In another pan, combine sugar and water to make the syrup.
  6. Add cardamom powder and rose water to the syrup.
  7. Once the balls are fried, soak them in the warm syrup for at least 30 minutes.
  8. Garnish with chopped nuts if desired.
  9. Serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy your Gulab Jamun!
